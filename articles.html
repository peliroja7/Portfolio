3. What sort of documents do you have to provide?

Here we have the more technical part of the process. Make the most of your situation and gather as much evidence and information of your case as you can. This is the essential part of the process, and it will help clarify your case to your lawyers, but will also help you be more certain of the process.

Some of the most important documentation are the following:

  • relevant photos of the accident scene
  • photos of your injuries
  • medical records that will help prove your injury
  • official reports regarding your incident
  • police citations
  • laboratory test results
  • prescriptions you may have taken or still take
  • government issued IDs
  • doctor reports
  • witness information
  • receipts for any injury related expenses
  • your personal notes
  • any other relevant information

Like we said, there is no case just like the other, some of these documents may not apply to your case, which is completely fine.

If you want advice about your personal notes, please hear us out. The human memory can be, after some time, prone to deprive of quality details of your personal injury, so take our advice and write down your personal notes, so you won’t forget them over time, they are so important.

Of course, we understand you cannot remember every little thing and detail, but try to remember and write down a relevant chain of events that brought down the incident, who was there, how much time it took, how were you affected and were you in any way assisted.

Having this in one place will not only help in a big amount to your lawyer, but it will also up your chances of winning in court.

4. Florida state laws and evaluating if you have a case

This section is about the determination if you have a case. Every state has its own regulations concerning personal injuries. Here are some of the laws in the Florida state regarding personal injuries:

  • Dog bite cases: Florida has a strict liability policy regarding dog bites. The owner is held responsible, regardless of the dog’s past behavior.
  • Florida law allows injured victims to file a personal injury lawsuit after a traffic accident if it resulted in a serious injury to you. A serious injury is defined under Florida law to include any of the following types of injuries: a permanent injury, significant and permanent scarring or disfigurement, significant and permanent loss of bodily function and death
  • Product liability claims: Product liability arises when a person is injured by a defective consumer product. Florida applies strict liability principles to these claims, meaning that it imposes a duty on product manufacturers to make products safe.
  • To establish negligence, you must satisfy certain elements. You must first show that the defendant owed you a duty of care. For example, in a medical malpractice case, you must show that the health care provider owed you a duty to act according to accepted medical practices.

These are just some of the laws in Florida. After a free consultation with your lawyer, he will be the one to make the decision if you have a case.

The determination if you have a case can take some time, lawyers are busy people, they need time to get through the documentation and to re-evaluate everything.
